## Key Questions for Tax Counsel

1. **Classification Analysis:**
   - If the $16K was indeed a loan that was repaid in 2024, should a 1099 have been issued at all?
   - What documentation is needed to establish "loan" vs. "income" classification?
   - Does the form of repayment matter (cash, check, Zelle, offset against receivables)?

2. **Correction Process:**
   - Can Troy request a corrected 1099 directly from IS?
   - If IS refuses to correct, what is the IRS dispute process?
   - Should Troy file his 2024 return reporting the amount but contesting it (Form 8275 or similar)?
   - What is the statute of limitations concern?

3. **Evidence Requirements:**
   - What evidence would the IRS consider sufficient to reclassify?
   - Are contemporaneous documents (loan agreement, emails discussing loan) required, or can they be reconstructed from testimony?
   - Would bank records showing the transfer and repayment be sufficient?

4. **Interaction with Civil Claims:**
   - Can the 1099 dispute be joined with the state court action?
   - Or must it be resolved through IRS administrative channels separately?
   - Does filing a state court action for declaratory relief on the 1099 make sense?
