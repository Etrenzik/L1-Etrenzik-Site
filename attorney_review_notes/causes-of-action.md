## 1. Breach of Contract

**Theory:** IS failed to pay Etrenzik its agreed share (approximately 70%) of revenue from Gwinnett County Schools work.

**Elements (GA):**
- [ ] Valid contract (written or oral)
- [ ] Etrenzik performed its obligations
- [ ] IS breached by non-payment
- [ ] Damages resulted

**Evidence Status:** Alleged — need written agreement or corroborating evidence of oral terms.

**Open Issues:**
- Was the 70/30 split ever memorialized in writing?
- Were invoices formally submitted and acknowledged?
- What was the total amount paid by Gwinnett to IS for Etrenzik's work?

## 2. Account Stated / Open Account

**Theory:** IS acknowledged the debt (June 1 email: "submit invoices so books can be closed and amounts due paid") but failed to pay.

**Elements:**
- [ ] Prior transactions between parties
- [ ] Account rendered / balance struck
- [ ] Assent to the balance (express or implied by silence)

**Evidence Status:** Partially confirmed — June 1 email language is supportive but may not constitute formal account stated.

## 3. Unjust Enrichment

**Theory:** IS retained Etrenzik's share of Gwinnett revenue without legal justification.

**Elements (GA):**
- [ ] IS received a benefit (Gwinnett payments)
- [ ] At Etrenzik's expense (Etrenzik performed the work)
- [ ] Retention would be unjust

**Note:** This is an alternative/backup theory if contract claim fails due to lack of written agreement.

## 4. Tortious Interference with Business Relations

**Theory:** Sami Ali's appearance at Gwinnett County Schools with an attorney, alleging Troy was "stealing," interfered with Etrenzik's business relationship with Gwinnett.

**Elements (GA):**
- [ ] Existing business relationship (Etrenzik ↔ Gwinnett)
- [ ] Defendant's knowledge of the relationship
- [ ] Intentional interference
- [ ] Interference was improper
- [ ] Damages resulted

**Evidence Status:** Alleged — need Gwinnett witness statements and evidence of resulting harm.

---

## 5. Defamation / Slander

**Theory:** Sami Ali's statement at Gwinnett that Troy was "stealing" is a false statement of fact made to third parties causing harm.

**Elements (GA):**
- [ ] False statement of fact
- [ ] Published to third parties (Gwinnett staff)
- [ ] Concerning the plaintiff
- [ ] Causing special damages (or per se — accusation of a crime)

**Note:** Accusation of theft may be slander per se in Georgia — attorney should evaluate.
